A letter to the Python community in Africa

by Anthony Shaw, January 18, 2019
<p>Greetings from Australia,</p> <p>I am not an authoritative source on Africa. I am neither an African resident or national. I spend about 1‚Äì2 months of the year in South Africa for work but isolated into the office or my hotel.</p> <p>Over the last 12 months, I‚Äôve been enraptured with the creativity and passion of the Python community across the African continent and want to use this letter to explore and share some of the amazing things that are happening.</p> <p>I believe the broader international Python and Software community can learn a lot from what so many amazing people are doing across Africa.</p> <p>This is not a story of overcoming adversity as is often painted in the media, this is a story of opportunity and passion. In my experience, Africa is a colorful mosaic of cultures and history, the more you learn and explore, the more you get drawn in to go further.</p> <p>Speaking to non-Software people about Python in Africa, you might get some funny looks. The ‚ÄúAfrican rock python‚Äù is the largest known species of snake native to Africa, with an average adult length of 20 feet and lengths recorded up to 30 feet. If you are here for photos and stories of snakes, I‚Äôm sorry to disappoint but there aren‚Äôt any.</p> <h2>Stories from Southern Africa</h2> <p>‚ÄúSouthern Africa‚Äù, generally includes the nations of Angola, Botswana, Eswatini (Swaziland), Lesotho, Malawi, Mozambique, Namibia, South Africa, Zambia, and Zimbabwe. The UN‚Äôs definition would exclude Mozambique, Zambia, and Zimbabwe.</p> <p>Some of these countries are very small, Lesotho is a small, mountainous, land-locked country in the middle of South Africa. South Africa is a huge country with almost 60 million people and a GDP higher than Denmark. To say this is a diverse group of countries would be a gross understatement.</p> <p>There are some large metropolitan cities in Southern Africa, mainly Cape Town, Luanda, Johannesburg, and Durban.</p> <h3>Namibia</h3> <p>Namibia hosts the <a href="https://na.pycon.org/2018/">PyCon Namibia</a> conference in the capital city Windhoek. 2018 was the biggest PyConNA yet.</p> <p><img alt="photo from pycon namibia" class="img-responsive center-block" src="/img/posts/pycon-namibia.jpeg"></p> <p>The attendance of PyCon NA was 50% male and 50% female.
